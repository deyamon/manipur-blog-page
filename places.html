Places to Visit

From floating lakes to ancient forts and busy markets, each place tells a quieter side of Manipur.

Loktak Lake
The largest freshwater lake in Northeast India.
Boat rides Sunrise Nature
Keibul Lamjao National Park
The world’s only floating national park, home to the endangered Sangai deer.
Wildlife Floating park Sangai
Kangla Fort
Historic fort complex in Imphal, reflecting the region’s royal and colonial past.
History Architecture Evening walks
Ima Keithel (Mother’s Market)
A market run entirely by women, filled with local produce, textiles, and daily essentials.
Market Women-led Shopping
Hill Districts & Valleys
Ukhrul, Tamenglong and other hill regions offer trekking, waterfalls, and quiet villages.
Trekking Waterfalls Scenery
Border Towns & Beyond
Towns like Moreh open up trade and travel routes towards neighbouring countries.
Border town Trade Routes
Manipur’s landscapes blend floating worlds, ancient ruins, and quiet valleys — inviting every traveler to slow down and breathe.
